Comparison Analysis

Deceptive Techniques:

  • Truncated Y-Axis (75-95): Hides the fact that scores only improved by 15 points out of 100
  • Narrow Aspect Ratio: Makes the slope appear steeper than it is
  • Green Color: Implies "success" and positive outcome
  • Biased Caption: Claims causation without evidence

Ethical Corrections:

  • Full Y-Axis (0-100): Shows true proportion of improvement
  • Standard Aspect Ratio: Accurate visual representation of trend
  • Neutral Blue Color: No emotional manipulation
  • Honest Caption: Acknowledges sample size and correlation ≠ causation

Key Insight: Both charts show the exact same data, but the deceptive version makes a 15% improvement look like a dramatic transformation, while the ethical version shows it in proper context. This demonstrates how easily data visualization can be manipulated to support a false narrative.
